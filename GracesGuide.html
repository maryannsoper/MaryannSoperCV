Product Manager (Grace's Guide)

Applegate Nov 2010 - Jan 2015

Grace's Guide is the leading source of historical information on industry and manufacturing in Britain. This web publication contains over 173,000 pages of information and over 249,500 images on early companies, their products and the people who designed and built them. When I started, the project was one man’s hobby and I developed a scalable archiving process, created a team and enabled it to achieve charity status.

Digitally archiving The Engineer

I sourced all volumes of this prestigious journal from 1856 to 1950, digitally archived each page and indexed accordingly, to provide the first resource of its kind for historians.

Take a look for yourself →
  • I recruited and trained a small team of archivists.
  • I developed a bespoke digital archiving service; I established a reliable method for photographing and digitally recognising the text of delicate documents, which continues to underpin the team’s activity.
  • I built good relationships with private collectors of rare reference material and secured professional partnerships, which enabled the project to source the publications that make it unique.
  • I created a long-term business strategy that supported the charitable objectives and developed comprehensive funding proposals, including an £80k bid to the then Heritage Lottery Fund.
  • I redesigned the dated website using Google Analytics, peer research and user-feedback, focusing on user experience, while improving the technical framework of the archive.
  • I wrote, edited and proofread content and photographed exhibits and civil engineering, adding my unique content to the archive.
  • I provided mentoring for junior members in my team to enable them to develop digital and content management skills.
